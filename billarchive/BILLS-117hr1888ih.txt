
 1.  Short title This Act may be cited as the  Improving Access to Indian Health Services Act . 
  2.  Extension of full federal medical assistance percentage to Indian health care providers Section 1905 of the Social Security Act ( 42 U.S.C. 1396d ) is amended—
 (1)  in subsection (a), by amending paragraph (9) to read as follows:    (9)  clinic services furnished by or under the direction of a physician, without regard to whether the clinic itself is administered by a physician, including— 
 (A)  such services furnished outside the clinic by clinic personnel to an eligible individual who does not reside in a permanent dwelling or does not have a fixed home or mailing address; and   (B) such services furnished outside the clinic by any Indian Health Service facility, a health program or facility operated by a tribe or tribal organization under the Indian Self-Determination Act ( Public Law 93–638 ), or an urban Indian organization receiving funds under title V of the Indian Health Care Improvement Act; ; and 
 (2) in subsection (b), by inserting after  Papa Ola Lokahi under section 8 of such Act  the following: ; the Federal medical assistance percentage shall also be 100 per centum with respect to amounts expended as medical assistance for services which are received by an Indian Health Service facility, a health program or facility operated by a tribe or tribal organization under the Indian Self-Determination Act ( Public Law 93–638 ), or an urban Indian organization receiving funds under title V of the Indian Health Care Improvement Act.
