 1.  Short title This Act may be cited as the  Concerns Over Nations Funding University Campus Institutes in the United States Act  or the  CONFUCIUS Act .
 2.  Restrictions on Confucius Institutes  (a)  Definition In this section, the term  Confucius Institute  means a cultural institute directly or indirectly funded by the Government of the People’s Republic of China. (b)  Restrictions on Confucius Institutes An institution of higher education or other postsecondary educational institution (referred to in this section as an  institution ) shall not be eligible to receive Federal funds from the Department of Education (except funds under title IV of the Higher Education Act of 1965 ( 20 U.S.C. 1070  et seq.) or other Department of Education funds that are provided directly to students) unless the institution ensures that any contract or agreement between the institution and a Confucius Institute includes clear provisions that— (1)  protect academic freedom at the institution;  (2)  prohibit the application of any foreign law on any campus of the institution; and  (3)  grant full managerial authority of the Confucius Institute to the institution, including full control over what is being taught, the activities carried out, the research grants that are made, and who is employed at the Confucius Institute.
